Peter Thiel

The Founder's founder

There are tech entrepreneurs and there are the tech architects of the future. Peter Thiel single handedly defines the latter. Born in Frankfurt Germany and raised in Calofornia, Peter Thiel is an epitome of the depths and levels in which a curious and visionary mind can reach. Recognised as one of the most influencial men in the world as the co-founder of paypal and palantir. Peter Thiel has been a key figure and inspiration to many in the tech and business industries. However, in order to understand the man we have to understand his philosophy and vision for the future. "what important truth do very few people agree with you on?" is a question that Peter Thiel asks himself and others. This question is the basis of his philosophy and vision for the future. He believes that in order to create something truly innovative and impactful, one must be willing to challenge the status quo and think differently from the majority. This mindset has led him to invest in and support many groundbreaking companies and ideas, such as SpaceX, Facebook, and Palantir. Peter Thiel's impact on the tech industry and his vision for the future continue to inspire and shape the world we live in today. A deeper understanding of peter Thiel comes from his expressiveness in his book "Zero to One". In this book, Peter Thiel shares his insights and experiences on how to create a successful startup and build a better future. He emphasizes the importance of creating something new and unique, rather than simply copying existing ideas. He also stresses the importance of having a clear vision and mission for your company, and being willing to take risks and challenge the status quo. Overall, "Zero to One" is a must-read for anyone interested in entrepreneurship and innovation, and it provides valuable insights into the mind of one of the most influential tech entrepreneurs of our time. Love him or challenge him, one thing is for sure, the world thinks differently because Peter Thiel provided an alternative perspective. Indeed a great tragedyis to leave a great unacknowledged while he still lives among us. let this be his roses, given now while he can still receive them. Although history will remember him, we should not wait for history.
